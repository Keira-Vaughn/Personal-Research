Criminal Law

Criminal Law is the branch of law that defines criminal offences, regulated criminal apprehension, charging, and trial of suspects and decides the penalties and treatment applicable to convicted offenders.
PRINCIPLES OF CRIMINAL LAW
Originally, the approach to criminal law was that a crime is an act that is morally wrong. Now, here in modern times, many consider the purpose of criminal law is to be the prevention of crime. In 1962 it's stated by the American Law Institute that the objective of criminal law is to give fair warning of the nature of the offense and to promote the correction and rehabilitation of offender
